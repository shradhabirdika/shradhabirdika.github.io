About Me

  • I am a dedicated GIS professional passionate about leveraging geospatial technology to enhance environmental management and urban planning. With an accelerated Master's in Geographic Information Science (GIS) from Clark University, I bring a strong academic foundation, hands-on experience in spatial data analysis, and a commitment to sustainable development.

    Currently, I am a student intern at the Massachusetts Department of Environmental Protection's Total Maximum Daily Load Section, where I develop comprehensive databases and GIS-based maps to support watershed management and environmental planning. My background also includes being a teaching assistant for Python programming for GIS at the graduate level, managing interior mapping projects, and conducting research on urban heat islands and environmental justice.

    I thrive in roles where I can use my skills in GIS, data visualization, and analysis to solve complex problems and drive impactful change. Proficient in tools like ESRI ArcGIS, Google Earth Engine, RStudio, and AutoCAD, and fluent in Python and Java, I am eager to connect with professionals and organizations committed to using geospatial insights for sustainable development.

    I welcome any opportunity to connect and/or collaborate on projects based on GIS applications or environmental consulting!
